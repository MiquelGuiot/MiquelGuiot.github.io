Bones! I am Miquel Guiot Cusidó, a PhD student in Cryptography at the [Computer Engineering and Mathematics Department](https://www.deim.urv.cat/en/) of [Universitat Rovira i Virgili](https://www.urv.cat/en/), Tarragona (Catalonia), advised by [Oriol Farràs](https://sites.google.com/site/oriolfv/).

I started my PhD in January 2024. Before that, I was a Research Engineer at [Barcelona Supercomputing Center](https://www.bsc.es/) working in High-Performance Computing (HPC). I obtained a MSc in Advanced Mathematics at [Universitat de Barcelona](https://web.ub.edu/en/home), Barcelona (Catalonia). Formerly, I completed a BSc in Mathematics and a BSc in Computer Science at the same university.

My work focuses on the study of <b>secret sharing schemes</b>, a cryptographic primitive. Furthermore, I am also interested in all areas of <b>cryptography</b> and its relation to <b>complexity theory</b> and <b>HPC</b>. Feel free to reach out if you are also interested!

Outside of research, I like reading novels and short stories, watching movies, road cycling, and football (especially Barça!), and I enjoy discussing them passionately.
